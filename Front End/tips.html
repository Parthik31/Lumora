For New Solar Buyers

Right System Sizing

Based on average Indian households, we recommend starting with a 3kW system which can typically cover 70-80% of electricity needs for a family of 4.

Vendor Selection

Always choose MNRE-empaneled vendors for quality assurance and to be eligible for subsidies. Check vendor ratings on the national portal before finalizing.

For Existing Solar Owners

Battery Maintenance

For lead-acid batteries: Keep water levels topped up, clean terminals regularly, and avoid complete discharges to extend battery life by 2-3 years.

Panel Cleaning

Clean panels every 2 weeks during summer and monthly in monsoon. Use soft cloth and plain water - avoid harsh chemicals that can damage anti-reflective coating.

Financial Optimization

Net Metering Strategy

Shift high-consumption activities (like washing machines, geysers) to daylight hours when solar generation is peak to maximize self-consumption and minimize grid export.

Subsidy Updates

The current 30% central subsidy can be combined with state subsidies in some regions (like Kerala, Gujarat). Check your DISCOM website for local incentives.

Weather-Based Tips

Monsoon Preparation

Before monsoon: Check all mounting structures for rust, ensure proper earthing, and trim nearby trees to prevent shading and physical damage during storms.

Summer Optimization

In peak summer, clean panels more frequently as dust accumulation can reduce output by 15-20%. Consider adding a reflective surface behind panels to boost yield.

Space-Constrained Solutions

Vertical Installations

For homes with limited roof space, consider vertical installations on walls or balconies. While less efficient, they can still provide 60-70% of rooftop output.

Community Solar

If your rooftop is unsuitable, explore community solar options where you can invest in a shared solar plant and get credits on your electricity bill.

Performance Monitoring

Reading Your Inverter

Check your inverter display daily: Green light = normal operation, Yellow = reduced output (check for shading or dirt), Red = fault (contact installer).

Monthly Health Check

Compare your monthly generation with our benchmarks: 3kW system should produce ~360 units in summer, ~240 in monsoon, and ~300 in winter (North India).
